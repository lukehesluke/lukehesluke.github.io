Euclidean Construction Design

This is my current obsession: the relationship between Euclidean construction and traditional design across the world.

I've created a very simple programming language for describing Euclidean constructions, and will use this to explore how some of these designs could have been (or could be) constructed. Later, I hope to also explore the use of extensions to Euclidean construction (e.g. origami) on designs.

Euclidean Construction

Euclidean construction is a method for constructing figures on a canvas with just two tools: a straightedge and a compass.

Two points

You start with two points [1]:

From this starting point, you can construct anything [2].

Circles

Place the compass with one end on one point and the other end on another point and you can draw a circle.

Lines

Use the straightedge to draw a line through two points.

Or a line segment between the two points.
